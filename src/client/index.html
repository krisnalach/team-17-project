How It Works

BlitzJack is played with one player and one dealer.

The player is dealt two cards and the dealer is dealt one card face up and one card face down.

Numbered cards are worth their face value, face cards are worth 10, and aces are worth 1 or 11.

The goal of the game is to get as close to 21 as possible without going over.

If you go over 21, you bust and lose the game.

If you and the dealer both have the same score, it's a push/tie.

If you get a higher score than the dealer without going over 21, you win.
How To Play

You start the game with 5 hearts. If you lose a round, you lose a heart. You can regain hearts by hitting certain score thresholds.

You as the player have 4 possible actions: Hit, Stand, Double, and Split.

Hit: Draw another card from the deck. You can hit as many times as you want until you bust or stand.

Stand: Keep your current hand and end your turn.

Double: Risk two hearts for a chance to score higher. Take one final card. Can only be done on the first turn.

Split: If you have two cards of the same value, you can split them into two separate hands. Risking one heart on each hand.
The Dealer

The dealer will always hit until they reach a score of 17 or higher. Then they will either stand or bust.

The dealer will also hit on a soft 17 (a hand with an ace valued as 11).

The dealer will stand on a hard 17 (a hand with an ace valued as 1).

The dealer will not split or double.
